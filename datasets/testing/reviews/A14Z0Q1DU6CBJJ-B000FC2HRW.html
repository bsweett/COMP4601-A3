There's something to be said about the FINAL DESTINATION 3 series. Strangely enough, most trilogies of the horror genre lose their production value and strength of character. Yet, instantly I was struck by the quality of the production.

Honestly, the FINAL DESTINATION movies are REALLY one dimensional. There's no Freddy, Jason or Michael. There's an invisible force. A myth of death. A myth of when it's your time, it's your time and death will get you one way or the other.

So there's no funny Freddy lines or chases. No scary faces. There aren't even any scary moments. Just... death scenes. Sort of like watching a fictionalized FACES OF DEATH. Sorta morbid now that I think about it. Yet, the characters are engaging, especially the lead character Wendy, played by relative newbie Mary Elizabeth Winstead (Ring 2 & Sky High).

The most noteable thing, as I've already mentioned, is the production value. The MONSTER ROLLERCOASTER scene is very cool. Never in my life has a movie rollercoaster scene gave me that artificial feeling in the gut like being on a real rollercoaster, but, although not a huge effect, I sort of felt it in this movie. They took a regular and small theme park rollercoaster and CG'd a MONSTER ROLLERCOASTER and it's very effective. It looked incredibly high.

It doesn't stop there. Nearly all of the scenes in Final Destination 3 are well done and engaging. The weight machine, the drive thru, the tanning beds, the warehouse & the carnival.

I enjoyed the movie. I really did. Is it classic stuff? No. Does it have strong entertainment value? Sure does. I'll certainly buy this... just to ride the Monster Rollercoaster over and over.
