Check reality at the door and The Sentinel will entertain you for a good hour and forty-five minutes. It moves fast; it's suspenseful and very well acted and directed.

There are two plot lines going on: A Secret Service agent is having an affair with The President's wife, and there is a conspiracy plot to assassinate The President. (This President is really down on his luck!)

The film is well put together, but the whole idea that someone in The President's close personal Secret Service entourage could actually be turned as a spy, not just as an informant, but as an assassin's aide is...well...far-fetched.

And that The President's wife could ever be out of the public eye or the Secret Service's eye long enough to start an affair is...well...just plain ridiculous.

Plus you have Eva Longoria as a special agent which is about as believable as Pamela Anderson was as a Hollywood bodyguard in V.I.P. (1998 - 2002 TV series).

That all said, The Sentinel was a good movie. I was entertained enough to want to keep watching without getting up for a break. Kind of like a Tom Clancy novel meets Desperate Housewives.

Rent it.
