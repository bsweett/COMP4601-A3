Set in the early 1950s, this is a story about the Los Angeles Police Department, organized crime, corruption, and three cops on a collision course.

Commanding Officer Lieutenant Dudley remarks that to be a detective in the homicide bureau it is necessary to be willing to 1) Beat a confession out of a suspect you know is guilty, 2) Plant evidence on a suspect you know is guilty, and 3) Shoot a suspect in the back that you know is guilty.

With the city police department this tough, how then is it possible for organized crime to exist in Los Angeles?

The many subplots keep the viewer engrossed in the overall view of corruption and the ongoing drama.

Disc Two features the making of the movie, cast and crew commentaries, and the many interesting commentaries by the joint writers and director.

An excellent movie.
