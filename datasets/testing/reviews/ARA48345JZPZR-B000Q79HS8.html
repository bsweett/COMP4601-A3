Just to add my two cents, I agree with the criticism of so many regarding the over-use of the shakey handi-cam. It was especially disconcerting in the theatre with the wildly cut chase at the end. The DVD is somewhat more comprehensible.

That aside, I give the film high marks for an interesting story in a tight script that deepens the Jason Bourne story a bit more and for the fast pace that held my interest throughout. The movie didn't drag and made its points quickly and persuasively.

Furthermore, I like the film a great deal because of Matt Damon, who is fully inhabiting the character of Jason Bourne, the superbly trained and ruthlessly efficient assassin. Looking very fit and every inch a guy that could go on auto-pilot while he kicks your ay, Damon conveys the dual nature of a man that is troubled by his lethal past and yet is fully capable of employing his skills when necessary.

While everyone fusses around about the next James Bond, I couldn't help but think this movie Jason Bourne is a lot closer to Ian Fleming's coldly able agent with the license to kill.

In short, I hope we haven't seen the last of Damon as Bourne. I liked the characters played by Karen Allen and Julia Stiles and wouldn't mind seeing them in future outings as well.

And, finally, in the next one, whoever directs please note that the camera can jump around sometimes, just not ALL the time.
