The Bottom Line:

No Country for Old Men is much better as a straightforward thiller (the scenes with Josh Brolin and Javier Bardem are electrifying) than as an elegaic comment on man's nature (the scenes with Tommy Lee Jones crawl) and I expect a little bit more from a Best Picture winner than a character who only exists to advance the plot (Woody Harrelson); it's a good film but not a great one.
