I first saw this movie in the theater back in 1979. I still have the original plastic popcorn cups to prove it.

At the time I thought it was slow and bombastic, and I was dissappointed that they rehashed "The Changeling" episode from the original series.

Well, perhaps I've mellowed a bit but I watched this DVD version last night a thoroughly enjoyed it. The key is to relax and soak in the experience. It's sort of a great, epic, mood piece with incredible visuals and a gorgeous sound track. Lower your expectations and let the movie come to you.

But beyond this, the movie poses a rather important question. What do you do if you spend your life searching for God, and you discover that God does not meet your pre-conceived notions of what He should be? V'ger finds that her "god" is a "carbon unit" and she doesn't believe it, but when the "carbon unit" enters the transmission code -- proving his claim to be "god" -- she puts her faith in him, and she joins herself to her "god" and becomes a greater being for it. There's a little to ponder there.

Beyond this, the documentaries on the second disk are interesting, and I enjoyed the various deleted scenes that showed the differences between this directors' cut and the original version. I haven't sat through the commentary track, but I'm looking forward to it.

Perhaps it wasn't all it could have been (and the documentaries explain a lot of why this is so) but I believe this movie has a lot to recommend it. I'll probably watch it again every few years until I transition to whatever the next life turns out to be.
