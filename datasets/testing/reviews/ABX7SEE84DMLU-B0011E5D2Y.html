This movie has a great premise. Yes, it requires a huge suspension of disbelief, but once you get over that reality barrier, it's tremendous fun.

Woven into it, though, are a few themes which are not quite so obvious:

1. Exploiting other people by lying is not attractive.
2. Rich people suck. Or they don't, depending.
3. In movies like this, everybody gets what they deserve.
4. Never play touch football at a politician's house.
5. Does Owen really look like he should be a family member at a ____ wedding? (Jewish, Irish, WASP, Greek, Italian - you pick).

The fun is totally infectious. My favorite scene: the opening where these two lawyers function as the funniest divorce mediators I've ever seen. And I could not stop laughing when we find out that the couple is in a contested divorce over frequent flyer miles.

Subtle, but really witty.
