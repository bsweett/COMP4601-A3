Unlike most of its peers, this film isn't as overdone with endless fighting and chasing, though there is plenty of that here. Instead, because it has to have some plausibility as being set during WW2, the effects here are not as headache-inducing. Yes, it has the usual evil-genius plot we would expect, but it also delivers some male bonding storyline, and a somewhat cliched strong-female love interest to relieve the hardware fantasy show. If you haven't seen how this fits into the grand scheme, you will still enjoy because the movie stands alone.
