I was skeptical about seeing The Hangover. After all, the previews weren't that great, and I was unfamiliar with the cast. But a friend of mine went to see it...then wanted to go see it again. I've never known this guy to pay for a movie twice, so I was intrigued. Could it be that could?

It was. The Hangover is perhaps the funniest film of 2009; arguably the funniest since Tropic Thunder. The film follows four guys--Ed Helms, Bradley Cooper, and Zack Galifianakis--as they take their buddy on a road trip before his wedding...then promptly lose him one drunken night. They wake up the next morning, their lives in a mess, their car missing, and a tiger in the bathroom. What follows is comedic gold.

All three leads are phenomenal, with near-perfect comedic timing. Supporting roles from contemporary farce mainstays Ken Jeong and Rob Riggle help round things out, while Mike Tyson's cameo is pitch-perfect. Todd Phillips, the director, also helmed Old School...which, while a funny movie, really shouldn't be compared to The Hangover. I haven't see a comedy like this in ages. I think you'll agree: for b*lls to the wall humor, The Hangover is worth every penny.
