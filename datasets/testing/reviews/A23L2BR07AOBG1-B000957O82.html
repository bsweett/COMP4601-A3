Because the movie serves as a list of actual information to men about what to do, or not to do if they want to have a good chance with women, or increase their percentage of success at least. As always, Will Smith is charming to no end and only helps men who really like women. This film has gotten some great reviews from friends of mine who think it's a very good film and while I understand why they feel that way (it's one of the best that have come out in awhile and it doesn't feel tired, or like a retread) but I still find the conventions of this genre annoying. As much as I enjoyed the relationship between Alex Hitchens and whatever her name was, I couldn't help but know that the writers had to throw a contrived obstacle in the way when it isn't really necessary.
