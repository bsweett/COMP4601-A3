...is not the cleverness of the conceit, nor the ingenuity used in representing the memory erasure, nor the flashing forward and back in time, nor any of the other story-telling devices. No, the real surprise is how sweet and touching is the love story that emerges through all of the imaginative convolutions.

With, perhaps, Jim Carrey's most sincere and accomplished "straight" acting job, matched in full by a fine performance by Kate Winslet, we finally get beyond adroit inventiveness in a Charlie Kauffman script, and get down to some real heart: very touching and ringing true.

Well done in all departments, it is the bittersweet longing for love in the eyes and actions of the leads that brings this film home. 4-1/2 stars. Well worthwhile.
