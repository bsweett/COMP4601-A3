"Brangelina" shooting big guns at each other didn't do it for me, no matter how beautiful they (the stars or the guns) are. My wife said it reminded her of "The War of the Roses" with Michael Douglas and Kathleen Turner, which I enjoyed. Other than the very basic premise of hubby and wife trying to kill each other, I couldn't see much in common between that clever and entertaining film and this very predictable star and firepower vehicle. Maybe it was intended as a date movie for dual markets--big guns for the guys and tabloid stars for the girls. It's appeal escaped me.
