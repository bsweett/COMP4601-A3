I had high expectations of this film, what with Will Smith, his son, and the down-on-your-luck-single-man-with-a-child bit. On the side of sentimentality it delivered the prerequisite emotional tugs to heart strings. But it really did nothing more than that. I was immensely impressed with the extra footage of the real Chris Gardner and his presence, and wished even more that Smith had embodied even a scrap of it. From an acting standpoint, it wasn't even a stretch for Smith, though I'm sure working with his son was a learning experience. I think he ran more in this film than any action film I've ever seen him in.
