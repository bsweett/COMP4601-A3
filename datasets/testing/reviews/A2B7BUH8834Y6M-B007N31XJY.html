Julia Roberts is superb in portraying the very real, very fiesty Erin Brockovich. Brockovich has a wit and intelligence that has been untapped in her struggle to make ends meet as a twice-divorced single mom of three.

Not only is it great to see someone down on their luck triumph, it's even more cathartic to see that person achieve success beyond their wildest dreams.

In the film, Brockovich is an endearing, caring mom, and a bristly, spitfire hot-head to those who are in her way.

She ends up being the instrumental researcher in a tainted water lawsuit that vindicates hundreds of families that were taken for a ride by the local water company - not to mention reaping great financial rewards for her lawyer employer.

Albert Finney is phenomenal as the attorney that hires her. His American is so flawless, it was hard to believe a British man was playing this role - not to mention his perfect performance.

Nearly every 4-letter word in the book is spouted out in this movie, so it wouldn't make a good gift to your kids, your mom or your church group. In addition to language, there is implied live-in sexual relationships that some parents may not want their children viewing.

For adults, however, this was an extremely entertaining film, especially when you realize it is a true story. Even the ending offers a surprise no one will expect.
