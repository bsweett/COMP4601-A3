Okay, the mark isn't very high, but this film still delivers what one expects from an action/conspiracy film.

**Spoiler Alert** I don't think I give anything away that Amazon and others haven't already, or even the DVD box itself, but I'd rather play it safe in the event I do give away more than one might like.

Amazon's review is accurate and to the point, so I won't belabor all the points of the plot here. Here is what works in this flick: Mark Wahlberg is wholly believable in this role that bares some resemblance to Matt Damon's Jason Bourne, and Danny Glover of the "Lethal Weapon" franchise fame makes a great villain along with Ned Beatty as a crooked senator. Michael Pena is cast perfectly as the FBI agent who sees more to the story than he's getting from his bosses. In addition, the direction is fast-paced and professional by the man who brought us Denzel Washington's Oscar performance in Training Day. The action is exciting and fun as these kinds of films are expected to have.

What doesn't work too well: The plot, which takes considerable "suspension of disbelief", goes down smoothly enough, but has holes you can drive a truck through. Wahlberg's girlfriend in the film is totally unbelievable, completely miscast, and poorly written. We are expected to believe that a girl who never saw a day of violence in her life can just pick up a shotgun and shoot a man to death while never blinking an eye. She even does so in her underwear. I think it's safe to say she is nothing more than eye-candy.

Okay, now why 4 stars and claiming it "hits it's mark" then, Steve? Well, it isn't aiming very high. It's a generally well-acted and executed action flick and should be judged as such. It isn't out to save the world, make a statement, enlighten us in any manner, and nor is it looking for Oscar nominations. It's simple popcorn fun.
