Plagued with post-production difficulties (evident in its truncated 97-minute length), "The Bucket List" managed to rake in $175 million worldwide. However, Rob Reiner's directorial skill deserves something more substantial than this manipulative piece of schmaltz. The film's selling point is the first-time pairing of Jack Nicholson and Morgan Freeman. Unfortunately, the Oscar winners display little chemistry in their scenes together. Nicholson overacts in desperation to keep the shuck and jive afloat, but "The Bucket List" is dead on arrival.
