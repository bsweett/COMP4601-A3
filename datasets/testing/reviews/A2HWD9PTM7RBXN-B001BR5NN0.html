This movie very accurately describes the trials and tribulations of five very different teenagers. Each of the five teens has critical issues that effect him or her and its is very interesting how each individual brings can bring out reactions from the rest of the group. Its almost like a teen therapy session and in the end, each person seems to learn from one another. Judd Nelson gives a real killer performance as a troubled teen trying to find happiness in a world that does nothing but dump on him. His humor seems to make him strong and impresses Molly Ringwald in the end. Its intersting to watch these five as well as the teacher and custodian evolve over the course of the movie. The depth of this movie plus aspects of both drama and comedy make The Breakfast Club one of my all time favorites.
