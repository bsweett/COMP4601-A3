This is far superior to most of the movies cranked out in this genre with some classic takes on gross out moments (though the girls playing a toilet version of the game "Battleship" almost had me wretching).

The actors playing Harold & Kumar are likeable and it's a nice turn to see stoners played with something resembling intelligence. The movie wisely pokes fun at itself and at just about every religious and societal group imaginable.

The movie is worth a watch if only for Neil Patrick Harris playing himself as a shallow Ecstasy tripping, sex-obsessed former star. It's nearly impossible not to break into hysterics at the idea of Dougie Houser getting aroused by (and involved in) a car seat.

Hardly great movie making, Harold & Kumar is still a helluva lot of fun. Especially under the influence.
