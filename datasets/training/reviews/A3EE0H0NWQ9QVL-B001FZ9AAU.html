`Iron Man' is awesomely cool,
U.S. forces and Afghanistan duel
From highlands and desolate caves
Stark Enterprises allegedly saves

Tony's the successor, a living prodigy
Of what weapons' development ought to be
Until selling his Jericho multiple warhead,
Ensuring the enemy will all but be dead.

Finding the enemy is well armed
By weaponry he never meant to have harmed
Our own side by enemy forces
Trickling in by unknown sources.

On his deathbed from an explosion,
Tony must produce from a dungeon,
A new Jericho weapon to beat all
Against U.S. troops and bring to a pall.

Now he makes himself the Iron Man
Defending the innocent whenever he can
From imminent danger of a major threat
Defending his company during the upset.

With a humanitarian touch, not based on money
And Pepper Pots, his helper, proves to be a real honey,
They face a greedy nemesis found in Stane,
Board-of-directors' man keeping investors sane.

See 'Iron Man' if you haven't yet.
Your entertainment value is a safe bet.

(The snappy dialogue and the awesome flying shots go well with the funny, slapstick test flight scenes from Tony Stark's Malibu laboratory. As others have often stated, Robert Downey, Jr. makes a fabulous, half-hapless hero. Gwyneth Paltrow is alluring and likable as his caring, but witty assistant, Pepper, and Jeff Bridges makes a welcome return as "Oby" Stane. Terrence Howard is believable as gung ho Colonel Rhodes. Directed with finesse by Jon Favreau.)
