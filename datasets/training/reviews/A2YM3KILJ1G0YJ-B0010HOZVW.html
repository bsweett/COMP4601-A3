Erica Bain (Jodie Foster) is the radio host of a pretentious program on some no doubt mind-deadening station. Her life is great and her eyes are closed to the real horrors of the city. But she soon has to watch her fiancé die at the hands of brutal murderers who also leave her in a coma and steal her dog. They steal her dog! Upon awakening, her world is rocked; she's borderline agoraphobic.

In a situation like this, people typically do one of two things. They either bury it deep inside, letting it burn, crippling them emotionally for years; or, they act out in a way that will help them overcome the trauma. Erica chooses the latter option, buying a gun, becoming revitalized, and seeking a complete resolution. Like Sally Field in Eye For An Eye, I'm VERY pleased with her choice.

From there she channels her inner Bronson and begins to clean up New York, one dirt bag at a time.

First up, a man enters a gas station, gets angry - more than likely about the fact that the clerk had trouble scanning a debit card - and blows his top like Michael Douglas from Falling Down if you added a strong meth addiction to the picture. Bain whips out her hand-cannon and fires from one aisle to another, through merchandise, to avenge the murder. Clean up, aisle three! As a follow-up she recreates my favorite scene from the Bernhard Goetz biography, and it's thoroughly satisfying.

The transformation Bain undergoes from safety to paranoia to judge/jury/executioner is what truly makes this movie great. Philosophical, political, emotional, physical, it's a metamorphosis in every way (similar to the 180 degree change Travis Bickle wanted for her in Taxi Driver). She may have thought that New York was one of the safest big cities before she was attacked - something she mentioned early on - but the experience has now opened her myopic eyes. Now, she plans on ensuring her previous misconceptions ring true.

This is, by far, my favorite Jodie Foster performance, and her performance is absolutely mesmerizing. Highly recommended. I can only hope that it turns into a series of duplicates like the original Death Wish Collection.
