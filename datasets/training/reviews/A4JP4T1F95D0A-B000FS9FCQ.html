While I didn't like this one as much as some recent graphic novel screenplays such as a History of Violence and Road to Perdition, it was a good try. I found it to be a bit long, and I thought it could have been trimmed a tiny bit. Though I know that Alan Moore fans are already angered by some of the side plots and character trimming that had already occured. The movie involves a future Britain that has become a Nazi like totalitarian state. There is no denying the jabs at the current American government. I am not a big fan of making terrorism a heroic thing, so I wasn't crazy about V blowing up things. It is an interesting movie and social commentary. Fine acting, and a fair adaptation. Worth a watch, but not quite a buy for me.
