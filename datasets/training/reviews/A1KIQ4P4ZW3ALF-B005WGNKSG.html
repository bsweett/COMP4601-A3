Signs of trouble:
(1.) English subtitles and audio don't match which indicates a lack of attention to detail by the producers; foretelling more issues to follow.
(2.) Heavy reliance on rape, incest, bondage, religious and Nazi fanaticism that didn't really seem plausible and were so disjointed that these pieces failed to meld together in a plausible manner. The writer/director know that the more controversy you can toss into a pot (plot) the more it sells.
(3.) Intellectual detective work seemed like pure imagination and lacked any believability especially the degree to which a low level sophisticated hacker easily and conveniently found obscure non-database linked information either on his/her computers or in their searches. It would take a lab full of CIA hackers/programmers, with the most sophisticated technology, months to get most but not all that was shown in this movie.
(4.) The story lacked depth and with only tidbits of history behind each character the story seemed like piecework. The result is that the viewer never feels as if they are a part of anyone's story. Just a viewing rabbit jumping from scene to scene.
(5.) The investigator and tattooed assistant made insights and connections between events that barely seemed plausible even to an omniscient viewing audience. Taping photos to a wall looks good on film but barely works in real life.
(6.) How does a hooded, overly pierced and overly dragon tattooed girl contribute to the storyline other than writer/director has an obsession with the bizarre and it sells to a sedated audience. The tattoo means?
