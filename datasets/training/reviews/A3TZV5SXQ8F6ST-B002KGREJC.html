What makes this film a classic is how the sterotypical settings of life are depicted in an originality devoid of stereotype; this is a path which truly can be seen, touched and felt.

While tackling the topic of teen pregnancy, the exploration of the daily race and grind - the track metaphor in the film is fitting - in what actually leads to graduating into adulthood is very real.

The trails in life are plenty and Juno is a wonderful portrait in motion.
