"Dark" is right: this is a dark movie with dark people and dark buildings where there is no daylight until the very end of the story. It's a combination sci-fi/film noir and takes place back in the 1940s or '50s in some big city.

A quick note on the Blu-Ray: it's 15 minutes longer with mostly developmental scenes from the early part of the movie added. The transfer is excellent, too, so it is definitely worth the purchase.

Story-wise, it's the usual alien nonsense but with some interesting twists you certainly haven't seen in other movies, like the city re-shapes itself every night.

Because of that, the set designs and special effect are unique. I know of no other movie, before or since (it's been 10 years), which shows a city with buildings expanding, rising, falling, etc. The visuals throughout this movie are fabulous and the sounds that go with it are good, too.

There is very little profanity in this movie but it's still a somewhat ugly, sordid story. Yet - at least with me - it draws you back for multiple viewings. There is a magnetism to this film. This is the only video I got rid of twice and re-acquired it both times....and then bought it a third time when this expanded Blu-Ray disc came out....and I'm glad I did. This is one more fascinating films of past decade.
