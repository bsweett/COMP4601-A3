After his family is murdered by a powerful drug lord (Steven Seagal), ex-Federale Machete (Danny Trejo) becomes a day laborer in the States until he is pulled into an underhanded plot to guarantee the election of an anti-immigration right winger (Robert DeNiro) to the Senate. Much bleeding and nudity ensues.

Writer/director Robert Rodriguez shares his colleague Quentin Tarantino's fascination with the exploitation cinema of the 70s, but Rodriguez has a purer vision. He may not elevate his material with frequently inspired dialogue, but he also does not distract from his story with awkward, self-indulgent homages. Rodriquez delivers a fast, entertaining, fun movie that one could imagine seeing in a grindhouse theater. Trejo is the Mexican Charles Bronson that we didn't know we were waiting for. It's a shame the guy is already 66 (!), because I'd like to see him crank out one or two hard and dirty thrillers a year for the next 20 years. Even if I didn't see them, it would be nice just to know they were out there. Seagal and DeNiro have a lot of fun with their roles.
