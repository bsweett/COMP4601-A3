Director Todd Phillips has done that rarest of things with this film--made an actually hilarious comedy, not one that is a corny embarrassment to both oneself and the people around them. Example:(Hot Tub Time Machine.

Four guys--Phil Wenneck (Bradley Cooper), Ed Helms (Stu Price), Zach Galifianakis (Alan Garner in an eccentric turn of brilliance as a sage-like manchild), and soon-to-be-married Doug Billings (Justin Bartha) decide to hit Vegas and have a wild time before their friend gets hitched. Not before encountering a very angry Mike Tyson, a sociopathic gay Chinese gangster, and a bunch of Indian guys who run a compromised church for intoxicated men looking to get "married".

Nearly every turn of dialogue in this movie is an exercise in self or-other deprecating black humor punctuated by strange moments of compassion. If he works it right, Phillips could be the future of contemporary comedy. There is a sequel in the works. This just about hits the mark of Kids in the Hall - Brain Candy and of course Office Space - Special Edition with Flair (Widescreen Edition). Not just recommended, this is a MUST SEE.

(I've heard rumors that actor Bradley Cooper is going to star in a remake of "The Crow". I sure hope that's not true, disrespectful as that would be to Brandon Lee's memory.)
