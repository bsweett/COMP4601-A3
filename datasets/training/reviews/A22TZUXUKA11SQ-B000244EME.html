In 1987's Predator, Arnold Schwarzenegger plays Dutch, a major in charge of a rescue team who handles the missions no one else can handle. When he is called on by his good friend Apollo Creed aka the General to find a missing cabinet minister who is lost in the jungle, he thinks it's because he's the best. He has no idea it's because this is really a suicide mission wherein they will be hunted by a sci-fi creature in the jungle who can blend in with the woods, has heat seeking guns on his shoulders, and hops from tree to tree like a monkey.

For some reason, this is the first time I've seen this movie and now that I've seen it, I'm a bit angry it took me so long. Predator is Schwarzenegger in his heyday--all brawn, accent, and arrogance. His team is just as impressive with a tracker who can sense the danger in the jungle, a tobacco-chewing redneck who "ain't got time to bleed," and, of course, Apollo Creed as the guy who got them all into this mess.

If you haven't seen Predator, this collector's edition is definitely the way to go. Not only does it have a sneak peek at Alien v. Predator, but it has an entire disc of extras with behind the scenes featurettes, outtakes, deleted scenes, etc. I would highly recommend this film to anyone who enjoys sction/adventure and sci-fi movies; it's the best of both worlds.
