Of that Wonka bar! Holla, my peepenheimers - I finally gave into my curiousity and watched the new "Charlie and the Chocolate Factory" directed by the highly overrated Tim Burton and the almost equally overrated Johnny Depp. PUH-LEASE!!

Look, I will not disguise my absolute unabashedly love for the original classic masterpiece Willy Wonka & the Chocolate Factory (Widescreen Special Edition). Gene Wilder is pure genious as Mr. Wonka, in a performance that simply cannot be repeated, bettered, or even equalled. Depp was foolishly ill-prepared to try. Whereas Wilder invested a truly menacing, disturbing side to Wonka, Depp makes him simply 'kinda weird'. Thumbs (and gobstoppers) down to him.

Much is always celebrated about Tim Burton's visual and overall atmosphere created in his movies. Yeah, he is a capable visual stylist. But it matters very little when he doesn't do anything else right! I'm not saying he never has (I loved Frankenweenie). But more often enough he just comes across like a dork.

I liked Deep Roy as the Oompa-Loompa's (and he was also the film's narrator). But I thought the new Charlie was too goody-goody and he embarrassed his grandpa. After his Grandpa was happy about being able to go to the factory, Charlie was all "Oh but I should sell the ticket to help the family" and made his Grandpa look like a big jerk. There is a limit to how "helpful" a child should be. Talk about a killjoy. The actor playing the Grandpa is utterly delightful! Best performance in the film.

I didn't like the kids as much as in the original. I don't mean the young actors themselves, heavens no. I think Burton gave them one direction, something along the lines of: "Oh, you've all seen the original movie, right? Cool, just act like that." I don't blame them. Some of them talented, especially Anna Sofia Robb who shined in Because of Winn-Dixie. But I don't know why they used a digital fake for Agustus Gloop instead of a real boy!
