ALICE IN WONDERLAND (2010) had an excellent surprise - for me, anyway - in the form of the beautiful, enchanting Mia Wasikowska as Alice. I don't care what this actress' age is: she is sexy, she is sweet and she can act. And boy, did she have to really act in this otherwise strange, lifeless film.

I know, I know: it's TIM BURTON! It's JOHNNY DEPP! It's a ton of British and other loveable voices from today's Great Age of Cinema! It's innovative in color, CGI animation and other nonentities. Ho-HUM!!

Why am I so icy about this film? Well, where to start. 1) Crispin Glover (THE DONNER PARTY) whom I have always loved deserves a better major comeback than this. 2) Alan Rickman could have found a different speech cadence than Severus Snape to play the caterpillar. Also ... enough of Helena Bonham Carter and her amateurish, over-the-top nonsense and one may say the same about Depp, by the way. Where is the Depp of SLEEPY HOLLOW?

The great Stephen Fry as the Cheshire Cat was one of the highlights of this rather boring and at times disturbing 'sequel' to the classic Alice in Wonderland story I know from my childhood. (Never liked it.) Michael Sheen was a very welcome voice; Anne Hathaway was downright creepy as she usually is. Christopher Lee, a lifelong favorite of mine, sounds too much like Count Dooku.

I'd only add: buy at your own risk. Though it has superlative special effects and some brightness of spirit, I didn't like it, but that's just me. So don't come throwing hate-mail-wrapped bricks through my windows.
