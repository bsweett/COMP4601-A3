Critics have been groaning about the unprecedented number of remakes on the 2005 Hollywood release schedule, but the first out of the bag, "Assault on Precinct 13," is not at all bad, and gives hope the year may be less oppressively redundant than it looks on paper.

The original was a low-budget 1976 siege thriller that John Carpenter made two years before his big commercial breakthrough with "Halloween," and which he claimed was inspired by the great Howard Hawks' westerns, specifically "Rio Bravo."

In this update (with which Carpenter apparently has no creative connection), the hero is a guilt-ridden, burned-out police sergeant (Ethan Hawke) in charge of a dilapidated, inner-city precinct that's about to be abandoned with the new year. But when a howling blizzard paralyzes the city on New Year's Eve, a police bus is diverted to the station and its handful of detainees includes a recently arrested gang chieftain (Laurence Fishburne) who intends to rat out his corrupt partners in the police department.

So, before long, the handful of cops working the holiday shift find themselves isolated by the elements, cut off from communication with the outside world and encircled by hostile forces intent on silencing the gangster and eliminating all witnesses.
